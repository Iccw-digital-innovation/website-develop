"A digital-twin platform for the water-health nexus in a rural village"


The aim of this project is to develop a digital tool to demonstrate the relationship between water quality and health in rural villages. We have chosen Nallampatti Town Panchayat, a village in Erode district, Tamil Nadu, as the study village as the incidence of non-communicable diseases, such as diabetes, hypertension, heart disease and cancer were found in published research studies to be up to twice as high as the average. Our project seeks to use data collected by sensors, samples, and surveys to create a dynamic replica of the situation, or digital twin, to conclusively establish and monitor the correlation between environmental factors such as water quality, soil quality, farming practices, and industrial activities in the region with the cases of non-communicable diseases. Once this has been achieved, appropriate solutions can be suggested by the digital twin and implemented in the field. The project has been funded by AquaMAP Centre, IIT Madras, and is being carried out by International Centre for Clean Water (ICCW), IIT Madras Research Park and their partners.
